You steady the lens. Wheat and weeds together — mercy in the fire’s language. The crowd murmurs a prayer you don’t remember teaching them. Heat lifts the hair on your arms as Nèamh flares; the oil darkens to a mirror where faces blur, names thinning to smudges.

“Do it,” says one voice. “Wait,” says another. They might be the same person. Muir shows you who begged, who stole, who carried grain to a hungry neighbor. Talamh drinks in their echoes. If you falter, the breeze will decide for you.
