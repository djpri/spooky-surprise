You lower the torch. The murmurs sharpen. Somewhere, a mirror turns its face to the wall. You try to steady the gathering with your breath alone — but breath is a kind of wind, and wind loves to feed fires.

“If not the pyres,” someone says, “then what?” Your veins answer with a warning pulse. The Veil leans closer to hear your reply.
