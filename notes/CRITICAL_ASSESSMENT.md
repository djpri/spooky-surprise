### 1. **Reflection Branch Lacks a "Integration" Victory**
**Issue:** Reflection branch has *one* broadly positive ending: [5B6] "Use the Record to Break the Cycle" (Transcendent).

- **Problem:** Feels tacked-on compared to Corruption, which has two "good" endings ([5C13] Integration, [5C10] Earned Balance).
- **Consequence:** Reflection ending tone-distribution is: 1 Good, 5 Ambiguous-Dark/Traumatic. Very bleak.
- **Fair?** Maybe‚ÄîReflection is about mirrors, fracture, dissociation. But it creates asymmetry.

**Suggestion:** Either:
- **A) Add a second Reflection "good" ending** tied to reconciling with the Archivist or the Mirror Child in a non-destructive way.
- **B) Lean into the bleakness intentionally** and add thematic justification (e.g., "Reflection is the branch where identity itself is at stake; redemption is rarer").

### 4. **Lenses Feel Underexplored as Narrative Tools**
**Issue:** The three lenses (N√®amh, Muir, Talamh) are mechanical/systemic but not deeply integrated into character voice.

- **Current state:** Lenses are used for solving problems (burning spirits, seeing truth, binding).
- **Problem:** They don't feel like *choices that shape personality*. Using Talamh doesn't make the protagonist feel differently about themselves; it just deepens marks.
- **Consequence:** The "cost" is visible but not *internalized*.

**Suggestion:** Lens choices could affect internal monologue/reflection scenes:
- High N√®amh use: Player becomes judgmental, sees things in binary (clean/corrupted).
- High Muir use: Player becomes melancholic, sees reflections of others' pain.
- High Talamh use: Player becomes hungry, hears whispers of renewal through consumption.
- This makes the lenses feel like they're *shaping the Brichan's mind*, not just the mechanics.

---

### 5. **Supporting NPCs are Thin Outside Their Encounters**
**Issue:** Sister Maerwyn, Mr. Harriot, Archivist of Ash are memorable *in scenes* but feel like encounters, not relationships.

- **Current state:** They appear, serve their narrative function, vanish. No ongoing presence.
- **Problem:** Relationships don't deepen. No sense of the protagonist being *embedded in a community*.
- **Consequence:** The world feels like a sequence of boss encounters, not a place with texture.

**Suggestion:** Add NPC "callback" scenes:
- Late in branches, player might encounter an NPC again in a changed context (Sister Maerwyn's faith visibly shaken; Harriot's jovialness cracking; Archivist revealing something deeply personal).
- These don't need to be major branching points‚Äîjust moments that show characters have *agency and arcs* parallel to the player's.
- **Scope impact:** Low-Medium (1-2 optional scenes per branch)

---

### 6. **Corruption's Bound Spirit Choice Could Echo Forward**
**Issue:** With Voice & Bride merged, Bound Spirit choice feels local and non-consequential.

- **Current state:** Player chooses to devour/free/bind the spirit; then moves forward. That choice doesn't resurface.
- **Problem:** Deepens "echo system" where earlier choices modulate later encounters.
- **Consequence:** Replayability is thinned‚Äîdifferent Bound Spirit choices feel identical across subsequent Voice & Bride encounters.

**Suggestion:** Add subtle modulation where Bound Spirit's fate affects Voice & Bride dialogue/tone:
  - **Devoured:** Guardians see predator; wary, aggressive.
  - **Freed:** Guardians see mercy-keeper; tempting, seductive.
  - **Bound:** Guardians see controller; resentful, testing.
- This is *tone shift only*‚Äînot new branching. No scope increase.
- **Scope impact:** Low (dialogue edits, no new scenes)
